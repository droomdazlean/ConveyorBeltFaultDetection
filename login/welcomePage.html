Research Focus

This final year research project explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence with Prognostics and Health Management (PHM) systems to revolutionize asset reliability in challenging economic environments.

The study specifically targets the platinum mining sector in Zimbabwe, demonstrating how advanced IoT technologies and machine learning can significantly improve operational efficiency, reduce downtime, and optimize maintenance schedules in resource-constrained settings.

Innovation Impact: This research has the potential to transform maintenance practices across the entire mining industry in Southern Africa.

Primary Objectives

Strategic Goals for Asset Reliability Enhancement

01

AI Model Design

Design and implement an advanced AI model that accurately predicts conveyor belt system failures within platinum metal mining organizations in Zimbabwe, utilizing multi-sensor data fusion and machine learning techniques.

02

MTBF Enhancement

Increase Mean Time Between Failure (MTBF) of conveyor systems, thereby improving Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) and plant utilization by at least 5%, directly impacting operational profitability.

03

Maintenance Optimization

Develop an AI-driven predictive maintenance scheduling system that optimizes maintenance actions, reducing unplanned outages by at least 5% while maximizing equipment lifespan and performance.

System Aim

The primary aim is the use of an AI model integrated with Prognostics and Health Management (PHM) to significantly improve the Mean Time Between Failure (MTBF) for conveyor belt systems in Platinum Group Metals mining operations in Zimbabwe. This enables proactive maintenance scheduling and profitability enhancement through accurate prediction of Remaining Useful Life (RUL) of critical equipment.

System Components

Hardware & Technology Stack

ESP8266 Microcontroller

Function: Core IoT controller with integrated Wi-Fi connectivity for data processing, sensor management, and API hosting capabilities.

Specifications: 32-bit processor, 80MHz clock, 128KB RAM, 4MB Flash

16x2 I2C LCD Display

Function: Real-time status display showing system health, sensor readings, alerts, and operational parameters for on-site monitoring.

Interface: I2C communication protocol reducing pin usage and simplifying connections.

Active Buzzer

Function: Audible alert system providing immediate notifications to operators during critical fault conditions or threshold violations.

Alerts: Multi-level alarm patterns for different severity levels.

MPU6050 Accelerometer

Function: High-precision 6-axis motion sensor measuring vibration levels on X, Y, Z axes to detect abnormal belt behavior, misalignments, and wear patterns.

Range: ±2g to ±16g, 16-bit ADC resolution

HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Sensor

Function: Precision ultrasonic ranging sensor measuring belt distance and alignment with millimeter accuracy for detecting material spillage and belt positioning.

Range: 2cm to 400cm, accuracy ±3mm

Servo Motor

Function: Simulates conveyor belt movement and operations for system testing, calibration, and feedback mechanism validation during development phases.

Control: PWM signal control with 180° rotation range

Technical Specifications

Detailed System Monitoring Parameters

Roller Malfunction Detection

Indicators: Distance variations, jittery vibrations

Detected through inconsistent ultrasonic distance readings combined with irregular vibration spikes in accelerometer data. The AI model correlates these patterns to identify bearing wear, shaft misalignment, or roller surface damage.

Belt Wear & Tear Monitoring

Indicators: Progressive vibration level increases

Continuous monitoring of vibration amplitude trends identifies gradual material degradation. Machine learning algorithms predict remaining belt life based on wear rate calculations and historical failure data.

Material Spillage Detection

Indicators: Sudden distance measurement changes

Ultrasonic sensors detect material buildup or spillage events by identifying sudden deviations from baseline distance measurements, preventing blockages and maintaining operational efficiency.

Belt Slippage Identification

Indicators: Rapid vibration fluctuations, distance dips

The system identifies belt slippage through simultaneous vibration pattern changes and distance measurement inconsistencies, indicating loss of friction or tension issues requiring immediate attention.

System Accuracy

The AI model achieves 99.9% accuracy in fault detection through ensemble learning techniques combining multiple sensor inputs and validated against historical maintenance records.

Monitoring Frequency

Sensors collect data at 10Hz (10 samples/second) providing real-time monitoring capabilities. Data aggregation occurs every 60 seconds for trend analysis and AI prediction updates.
